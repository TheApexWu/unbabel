Q&A ANSWERS (keep these short)

"Why not WhatsApp/WeChat?"
-> Those are walled by language. Bengali group and
   Mexican group never cross. We merge the signals.

"How do you get users?"
-> One church, one legal clinic. They already have
   multilingual foot traffic and paper bulletin boards.
   We digitize what's already happening.

"What about fake reports?"
-> You can't fake cross-language corroboration.
   One phone = one alias. You'd need multiple SIMs
   AND fluency in multiple languages.

"Isn't this Reddit?"
-> Reddit aggregates opinions. We aggregate evidence.
   This is epidemiology, not social media.

"Who pays?"
-> City agencies subscribe to the signal feed.
   Tenant advocacy orgs get real-time alerts.
   We never charge immigrants.

"What about liability?"
-> Section 230. Signals say "N reports mention X."
   That's a fact about reports, not a claim about
   the entity. PII stripped. Posts expire in 7 days.

"AI-generated attacks / fake accounts?"
-> One phone = one deterministic alias. Physical SIM
   required. The AI independently extracts entities --
   you can't control what it matches. Cost of attack
   exceeds any single-language platform.

"What's the technical moat?"
-> Single-inference multi-task pipeline. Cross-lingual
   entity resolution. The AI is infrastructure, not
   the product. Users never see a chatbot. They see
   patterns that emerged from aggregate data.

"Monetization?"
-> Three paths. City agencies subscribe to signal feed.
   Advocacy orgs pay for real-time alerts. Entity
   reputation ledger (neighborhood Glassdoor).
   Never charge immigrants.

"Learning to be American?"
-> Being American isn't learning English. It's learning
   that your voice combines with strangers' voices to
   create power. First amendment in every language.
